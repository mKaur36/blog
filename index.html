Sikh Takhts

Five takhts includes five Gurudwaras (Sikh Temples) representing the collective power of the Khalsa panth.


Sri Akal Takht Sahib Ji

Sri Akal Takht Sahib ji

Sri Akal Takht Sahib ji si Located in Amritsar, Punjab. Akal Takht Sahib Ji, the word "Akal" means timeless and "Takht" means Throne. Akal Takht Sahib Ji, the throne of temporal and eternal authority was built by the sixth Guru, Sri Guru Hargobind Sahib Ji. It was built in the year 1609 with the help of two Gursikhs Baba Buddha Sahib Ji and Bhai Gurdas Ji. Sri Guru Hargobind Sahib Ji used to wear two swords one for "Miri" (representing temporal power) and the other for "Piri" (representing spiritual power). Decisions for the Sikh Panth are taken at the takth and Sikhs all over the world are bound to follow them.

Takht Sri Patna Sahib

Takht Sri Patna Sahib

Takht Sri Patna sahib is the second Takht of the Sikhs and is located in Patna, Bihar.Tenth Guru Sri Guru Gobind Singh Ji was born here. Guru sahib Ji was a true leader of the Sikh faith. A divine messenger, a warrior, a poet and a philosopher, Guru Gobind Singh Ji molded the Sikh religion into its present shape, with the institution of the Khalsa fraternity and the completion of the Guru Granth Sahib Ji, Sikh's eternal Guru in the final form.

Takht Sri Kesgarh Sahib

Takht Sri Kesgarh Sahib

Gurdwara Shri Kesgarh sahib is located in Anandpur Sahib, Punjab. The city was Founded by Guru Tegh Bahadur Sahib Ji in 1665 named as Chak Nanaki. Later, Guru Gobind Singh Ji gave this city a new name Anandpur which means "City of Bliss". It was here that Guru Ji baptised the first five Sikhs and thus initiating Khalsa Panth. All the males were given Singh as their last name meaning "Lion" and Females were given kaur as the last name meaning lioness/princess.

Takht Sri Damdama Sahib

Takht Sri Damdama Sahib

Takht Sri Damdama Sahib, also known as Guru ki Kashi, is located in the village Talwandi sabo, 28 Km southeast of Bathinda. Guru Gobind Singh Ji stayed here after the sikhs fought several fights. Guru Sahib Ji before leaving towards Deccan blessed this city as Guru ki Kashi(Education Institution). This title was given because of the literary activities that Guru Gobind Singh Ji did during his stay. Guru Sahib compiled Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji by dictating bani to one of sikh disciples Bhai Mani Singh Ji Guru ji also added the hymns of the ninth Guru Tegh Bahadur Sahib ji. Damdami Takal(Education Institution) was also founded by Guru Gobind Singh Ji during their visit to Sabo ki Talwandi. Baba Deep Singh Ji was installed as the first Jathedar(head) of the Damdami Taksal(School for teaching Gurbani meanings and pronounciation).

Takht Sachkhand Sri Hazur Sahib

Takht Sachkhand Sri Hazur Sahib

Takht Sachkhand Sri Hazur Sahib is located in Nanded, Maharashtra on the banks of the river Godavri. This is the place where tenth Guru Gobind Singh Ji left his earthly life. The gurudwara within the complex is known as sachkhand meaning "Realm of Truth". Maharaja(king) Ranjit singh built this gurudwara. Baba Banda Singh Bahadur Ji also had his monastery in Nanded before meeting the Guru Sahib ji. Guru Sahib Ji blessed Baba Banda Singh Bahadur with five arrows for the battles ahead with the Mughal Empire. It was in Nanded when Guru Ji announced Guru Granth Sahib Ji the eternal guru.
